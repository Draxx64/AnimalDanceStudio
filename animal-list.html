Animal List

Giraffe

Giraffes are the tallest animal in the animal kingdom standing up to 14 to 18 feet tall thanks to their long neck. Their neck comprises of seven neck bones; the same amount as humans.

Monkey

Monkeys are adept tree dwellers and climbers. They are able to use their tail as a fifth limb to traverse the treetops quickly.

Cat

Cats are able to retract their claws, kind of like a sheathe. This is so they can keep their claws as sharp as possible.

Dog

Dogs have an incredible sense of smell. Their noses are at least 40 times more effective than human noses.

Frog

Because of their long hind legs, frogs have an amazing leaping ability and can leap over 20 times their body length.

Bear

Brown bears have claws that are over two inches long and can be up to over four inches long. Their claws are used for digging for food and self-defense.

Gorilla

Gorillas are the largest primate in the world. They can stand up to four to six feet tall and can weigh nearly 500 pounds.

Crocodile

Saltwater crocodiles have an incredible bite force of 3,700 pounds per square inch (psi) or 16,460 newtons. That's around the weight of a small car!

Armadillo

Armadillos are covered in a hard amor-like shell that covers their back, head, legs, and tail. When they feel impending danger, they curl up into a ball where only their armor is exposed, protecting them from predators.

Raccoon

Raccoon paws closely resemble human hands and can be used to easily manipulate food and objects.
